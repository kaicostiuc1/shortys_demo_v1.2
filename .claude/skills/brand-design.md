# Shorty's Diner — Brand & Design Rules

## What This Is
A classic American diner in Williamsburg, VA. Est. 1980. Real food, no frills.
This is not a tech product, not a fast-casual chain, not a modern restaurant.

## Tone
- Authentic and unpretentious — "it's just the way it used to be"
- Warm, local, community-rooted
- Never corporate, never polished, never trendy
- Copy should feel like it was written by someone who actually works there

## Visual Identity
- **Colors:** Red (`#e11d48`), cream (`#FBF9F4`), tan (`#f2ede4`), white. These are fixed.
- **Checkerboard:** Black and white only for header/footer bands. Red and white for section dividers (`Checker` component).
- **Fonts:** Boogaloo (cursive, headings), Playfair Display (hero title), DM Sans (body). Do not introduce new fonts.
- **Aesthetic:** Diner character — warm, worn-in, honest. Not minimalist. Not maximalist. Not "designed."

## What NOT to Add Without Being Asked
- Loyalty programs or sign-up CTAs
- E-commerce or cart functionality
- Dark mode
- Animations beyond what already exists
- Any copy that references "cheap," student discounts, or W&M specifically
- AI-generated placeholder content of any kind

## New Features — Guiding Principle
New features should feel like they belong in a 1980s diner that just got a website.
If it would look out of place on a laminated menu card, it probably doesn't belong here.
